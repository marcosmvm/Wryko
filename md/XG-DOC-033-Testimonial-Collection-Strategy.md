## Collection Triggers

### When to Ask

| Trigger | Timing | Method |
|---------|--------|--------|
| **First Meeting Booked** | Within 24 hours | Slack/email celebration + ask |
| **10 Meetings Milestone** | At milestone | Personal call |
| **End of Pilot (90 days)** | Day 85 | Formal request |
| **6-Month Anniversary** | Month 6 | Personal outreach |
| **After Positive NPS** | Score 9-10 | Immediate follow-up |
| **After Renewal** | At renewal | Gratitude + ask |
| **After Referral** | When they refer | Recognition + ask |

### Who to Ask

**Best Candidates:**
- NPS score 9-10
- Booked 10+ meetings
- Renewed or expanded
- Made unsolicited positive comments
- Referred other clients

**Avoid Asking:**
- Clients with ongoing issues
- Recently had complaints
- Below target performance
- In first 30 days (too early)

## Collection Process

### Step 1: Identify Candidates

**Weekly Review (Friday):**
1. Pull NPS scores from past 2 weeks
2. Review meeting milestones
3. Check for positive Slack/email comments
4. Note any referrals made
5. Add qualified candidates to Testimonial_Pipeline

### Step 2: Warm-Up

Before asking for a testimonial, ensure:
- Recent positive interaction
- No open support issues
- Results are strong
- Relationship is solid

### Step 3: Make the Ask

**Email Template (Written Testimonial):**

```
Subject: Quick favor? (2 minutes)

Hi {{first_name}},

I wanted to reach out because you've been seeing great results -
{{meetings}} meetings in {{duration}}. That's awesome!

Quick favor: Would you mind sharing a brief testimonial about
your experience with XGrowthOS? Just 2-3 sentences is perfect.

A few questions to spark ideas:
- What problem were you trying to solve?
- What results have you seen?
- What's been your favorite part of working with us?

Happy to draft something based on our conversations if that's
easier - you can just approve/edit.

Either way, thanks for being a great client!

Marcos
```

**Email Template (Video Testimonial):**

```
Subject: Would you share your story? (15 min)

Hi {{first_name}},

I have a special request - and totally fine to say no.

Your results with XGrowthOS have been exceptional:
- {{meetings}} meetings booked
- {{reply_rate}}% reply rate
- {{outcome}} achieved

Would you be open to recording a quick video testimonial?
It would help other companies like {{company}} understand
what's possible.

Here's what it involves:
- 15-minute Zoom call (I'll guide the conversation)
- 5-6 questions about your experience
- We handle all editing

As a thank you, I'd be happy to [offer: credit, gift, etc.].

No pressure at all - let me know what you think.

Marcos
```

**In-Person Ask (Best for Video):**

"Hey [Name], I have to say - your results have been fantastic. [Specific numbers]. Would you be willing to share that story in a quick video? It would really help other [industry] companies see what's possible. I can make it super easy - just a 15-minute call where I guide the conversation."

### Step 4: Capture

**For Written Testimonials:**
- Record verbatim (don't paraphrase)
- Get approval for exact wording
- Confirm name, title, company can be used
- Get photo if possible

**For Video Testimonials:**

Video Call Structure (15 min):
1. Intro & rapport (2 min)
2. Background question (2 min)
3. Problem question (3 min)
4. Solution question (3 min)
5. Results question (3 min)
6. Recommendation (2 min)

Questions to Ask:
1. "Tell me about {{company}} and what you do."
2. "What was happening with your outbound before XGrowthOS?"
3. "What made you decide to try us?"
4. "What results have you seen?"
5. "What's surprised you most about working with us?"
6. "Would you recommend XGrowthOS? To whom?"

### Review Response Protocol

**For Positive Reviews (4-5 stars):**
- Respond within 24 hours
- Thank specifically for feedback
- Reference specific point they made
- Keep it brief and genuine

**For Negative Reviews (1-3 stars):**
- Respond within 24 hours
- Apologize for their experience
- Take conversation offline
- Offer to make it right
- Follow up after resolution

## Reference Program

### Reference Call Guidelines

**When to Use:**
- Enterprise deals ($10K+/month)
- Prospect specifically requests
- Final stage of decision

**How to Set Up:**

1. Identify matching reference (industry, size, use case)
2. Ask reference permission first
3. Schedule call (keep to 20-30 min)
4. Brief reference on prospect's concerns
5. Let prospect lead the conversation
6. Follow up with reference after (thank you)

**Reference Call Prep Email to Client:**

```
Subject: Reference call request - [Prospect Company]

Hi {{client_name}},

We have a prospect ([Prospect Company], [industry]) who'd
love to hear about your experience with XGrowthOS.

Their main questions will likely be:
- [Question 1]
- [Question 2]
- [Question 3]

Would you have 20 minutes this week to chat with them?

I'll handle all the scheduling. Just let me know you're
open to it and I'll take it from there.

Thanks!

Marcos
```

### Reference Thank You

After a reference call:
- Immediate thank you (email/Slack)
- Small gift or account credit ($50-100)
- Update on outcome ("they signed!")

### Always Obtain

1. **Written permission** for use of name, title, company
2. **Approval of final content** before publishing
3. **Usage scope** (website, email, ads, etc.)
4. **Duration** (indefinite or time-limited)

## Key Takeaways

1. **Timing matters**: Ask when clients are happiest (milestones, NPS 9-10)
2. **Make it easy**: Offer to draft, keep asks short
3. **Be specific**: Testimonials with numbers are 10x more valuable
4. **Multi-format**: Collect written, video, and reviews
5. **Use everywhere**: Website, emails, sales, social
6. **Thank generously**: References are gold, treat them that way
7. **Track systematically**: Don't let good opportunities slip
